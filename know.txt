6. Conclusion

GitLab Community Edition (CE) is a powerful and comprehensive Software Configuration Management (SCM) tool that effectively supports version control, change management, and collaboration throughout the software development lifecycle.

Through this case study, various SCM concepts such as configuration identification, version control, branching, merging, change tracking, and auditing were successfully implemented using GitLab. Practical activities like creating repositories, committing changes, managing branches, handling merge requests, and tracking issues demonstrated how SCM principles are applied in real-world scenarios.

Additionally, advanced features such as issue tracking, wiki documentation, and tagging further enhanced the understanding of how GitLab supports complete project management along with SCM functionalities.

Overall, GitLab CE provides an integrated platform that combines SCM and DevOps capabilities, making it highly suitable for both academic and industrial applications. It helps in improving productivity, maintaining consistency, and ensuring efficient management of software projects.
